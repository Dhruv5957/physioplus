Q.2 What procedures are used in physiotherapy?

The assessment of the patient is done by a physio where they set a track for the process of the treatment. There are different approaches used in physiotherapy treatments, but they may vary from patient to patient depending upon the condition of the patient and the type of treatment needed. The common procedure includes the following: Manual Exercises Soft Tissue Mobilization -Electrotherapy-Heat Therapy -Taping for sports injuries - Ultrasound

Q.3 How is sports physiotherapy different from physiotherapy?

Improving sports player performance requires a deeper understanding of your body as a working system, and it’s this understanding that moves us way beyond simply reducing pain and into the realms of holistic treatment and long-term results. Physioplus sports physio starts by defining your goals and then moves on to understanding your pain or limitation, treating the cause (or causes, as there’s often more than one) and, crucially, staying with you on the journey to ensure lasting benefits. Physioplus sports physio even determines at an early stage that your shoulder injury, for example, needs investigation by another health professional before we can begin treatment. Or we might pick up in our early assessment that nerve irritation is causing muscle weakness and affecting your technique, which if undetected, would make a strength training programme a very ineffective treatment.

Q.4 Do I get relief from pain by performing the RICE (REST-ICE-COMPRESS-ELEVATION) procedure?

Yes, you will surely get relief but not be able to treat the source of your pain. To cure the source of pain, you need to consult a specialised physiotherapist to restore proper mobility by performing the right exercise routines prescribed by physio.
